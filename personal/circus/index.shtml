Dr. J Dana Eckart: Circus Skills

During my first quarter as an undergraduate at Georgia Tech I taught myself to toss juggle (balls first then clubs a bit later) and joined the newly formed juggling club the next (winter) quarter. The juggling club was a lot of fun and I learned a lot of different tricks. My two favorite activites though were "stealing" and "club passing", but alas you can't do either of these by yourself. If there's anyone local that would be interested in doing a bit of this once in a while, let me know. During my first year as an undergrad at Georgia Tech, I also learned how to ride a unicycle and bought one for getting around on campus. It was great to be able to bring my "transportation" into the classroom with me and just lean it up against my desk.
Many years later (about 1998) I built myself an ultimate wheel (essentially a unicycle without a seat) and was eventually able to go about 100 feet on it. About 10 years later, I bought a much better ultimate wheel than I had cobbled together and eventually got to where I could ride several hundred feet at a time (though most down a slight incline).
Occassionally I'll get out my toss juggling items and practice a bit, but really haven't done anything substantial in quite a few years. I have been trying to learn how to contact juggle, but alas I'm still quite the beginner. Guess it would help if I practiced more. At one time I used to joke that if the Computer Science career didn't pan out I could always run away and join the circus - but in retrospect it's a good thing it didn't come to that.
