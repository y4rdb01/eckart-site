Dr. J Dana Eckart: Advanced Programming Languages (CPSC 6129) - Regular Expressions

  1. Regular Expressions are frequently used to describe the various sequences of characters that make up the lexical elements (i.e., tokens) in a programming language.
  2. Regular expressions have the same expressive power as a Deterministic Finite (State) Automaton (DFSA or DFA).
  3. Because DFAs have no "memory" of what's been previously recognized, they (along with Regular Expressions since they are equivalent to one another) are unable to recognize something as simple as a palindrom of arbitrary length. While you can use the various states in a DFA to "remember" palindromes of a bounded length (say all those less than 5 characters long), you can't use them to determine whether or not strings of arbitrary length are in fact palindromes.
  4. Despite this limitation, regular exrepssions are well suited for recognizing the keywords, identifiers, literal values (e.g., numbers, strings), and comments found in programming languages.
