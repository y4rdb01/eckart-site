  1. Pandemic (as a class exercise)
    1. Each patch has a different population density, and thus contact rate between individuals, impacting disease transmission.
    2. There is an incubation period before an infected person is contagious. The incubation period has a minimum value, but thereafter there's a fixed chance of becoming contagious.
    3. There is a period of time before symptoms become apparent. If this time is greater than the incubation period, the difference is when the risk of disease spread is the greatest.
    4. There is a fixed illness recovery chance for infected patches.
    5. For each time step, there is a pre-set chance that a patch will become infected by a neighbor. The chance is 0 during the incubation period, greatest between contagious and symptom outbreak, and half the greatest value once symptoms emerge.
    6. For each time step during which a patch is infected and exhibits symptoms, there is a small but fixed chance of death in its population.
    7. Once a patch has been sick and recovered, it is considered immune from further outbreaks.
