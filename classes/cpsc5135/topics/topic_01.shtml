  11. Concerning Assignments
  12. Do NOT collaborate on assignments. While this seems unnecessarily restrictive at first, the following are my reasons (which my experiences have repeatedly confirmed):
    1. I won't know anyone is having difficulty with the assignment if everyone in class is talking to each other and no one bothers to come to me with questions.
    2. The other people you ask may know less about the assignment than you do. Even something as simple as what the assignment is asking for can be understood differently by different people. If you have questions, then the instructor (who, after all, created the assignment) will best understand the intent of the assignment and will be better prepared to help you along.
    3. Collaboration can be a slippery slope, and discussing solution approaches can lead to extremely similar code (depending on how extensive the discussions were) that gives the appearance of copying - a violation of the Academic Integrity policy.
    4. The graded assignments are part of how your understanding of the material is evaluated, and must therefore be your own work. When you collaborate too closely with others, the evaluation becomes that of many people's understanding of the material and not just your own understanding.
  13. In order to get the most benefit from the class notes and recorded lectures, I suggest printing out the class notes and then watch the videos, hand writting notes on the printed class notes. Based on research (abstract), this should better help you remember the material.
  14. Regarding the weekly quizzes, I strongly encourage you to use them as a study aid. In particular, read through the associated sections of the textbook first, then take the quiz while reading through the lecture notes for that week (and listening to the videos). After doing this, you will be very familiar with the material and should be able to ask specific questions in class concerning those elements you found difficult to understand. Also, please note that many of the quiz questions are multiple response - so you need to select all of the correct answers to get full credit.
