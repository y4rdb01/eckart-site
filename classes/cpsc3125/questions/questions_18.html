• Barriers are useful when a number of different threads/processes need to
    1. take turns working on a common task.
    2. must all meet at a common place before proceeding to the next phase of a computation.
    3. share a set of common variables/objects.
    4. none of the threads/processes are allowed to go past a certain point in the computation.
    5. None of the above

  • If all the threads/processes take very close to the same amount of time to reach the barrier, then a good way of implementing the barrier is
    1. spin locks.
    2. semaphores.
    3. monitors.
    4. message passing.
    5. None of the above
