The FHP Lattice Gas

This two-dimensional lattice gas uses a hexagonal neighborhood with only a single speed of particle. Each cell maintains information on up to six particles. Each particle must be traveling in one of the six hexagonal directions. Thus, no more than one particle can be incident to a cell from each of its six neighbors. Normally, particles pass through a cell undisturbed. However, when exactly two particles collide headon within a cell, they are randomly rotated either to the left or to the right. The which field of the cell is used for determining which of the neighboring cells are part of the hexagonal neighborhood. The presence of a particle traveling out of a cell is indicated by the particle fields, while the count field keeps track of how many particles were contained within a cell during the last time step and is used for displaying the results.
