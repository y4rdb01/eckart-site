Ising Model

This is a straightforward adaptation of the spin only Ising model presented on page 190 of Cellular Automata Machines: A New Environment for Modeling by Toffoli and Margolus (both of the Information Mechanics Group at MIT). The which field supports the alternating checkerboard lattice. The values of which are flipped each iteration in order to get alternation. The spin of a cell is flipped if and only if it has exactly half of its four (4) neighbors in an up (1) state and the other half in a down (0) state.
